Adapting to season

Native Plants | Winters in the Bay Area are mild, and summers are dry.

Tips:
- Winter: Plant native trees like oaks or manzanitas, and rain-tolerant shrubs.
- Summer: Use mulch to retain soil moisture. Avoid planting new lawns.
- Adjust your irrigation schedule in spring and fall depending on rainfall patterns.

Native Plant Selection

Native Plants | Native plants require less maintenance and adapt better to local conditions.

Recommended California Natives:
- Ceanothus (California lilac)
- Artemisia californica (coastal sagebrush)
- Eriogonum fasciculatum (California buckwheat)
- Toyon (Heteromeles arbutifolia)
- California poppy (Eschscholzia californica)

Pest control

Native gardens attract fewer pests, but some still pose a threat.

- Attract beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings) by planting dill, basil, and yarrow.
- Use natural traps (beer for slugs, potassium soap for aphids).
- Inspect regularly in spring and summer for pests like mealybugs or aphids.

Efficient Watering

Summer water restrictions and frequent droughts are common.

- Use drip irrigation instead of sprinklers.
- Water before 9 a.m. or after 7 p.m. to reduce evaporation.
- Install soil moisture sensors or smart systems like Hydrawise or Rachio.
- Group plants by similar water needs (hydrozoning).
